Mehndi, commonly referred to as henna, is a type of body art that has been used for ages by many different civilizations all over the world.
Henna has a long history and continues to enchant people with its elaborate designs and natural beauty,from the ancient civilizations of Egypt
and India to modern celebrations in the Middle East and North Africa. We'll look at the history of henna, its cultural significance, and how it's
become a well-liked trend in today's fashion and beauty in this post.

Services We Offer

Henna services typically involve applying henna paste to the skin, letting it dry, and then scraping it off. This procedure leaves behind a temporary stain that, depending on the type of henna used, where it is applied on the body, and how well it is cared for, can last anywhere from a few days to a few weeks.

Bridal Henna

Bridal henna, also known as bridal mehndi, is a popular service for brides-to-be. It involves applying henna designs to the hands and feet of the bride, often with elaborate and detailed patterns, as part of wedding traditions in many cultures.

Henna for Pregnant Women

Henna is often used to create beautiful belly designs for pregnant women, also known as "belly blessings" or "baby bump henna." It is a way to celebrate pregnancy and create lasting memories.

Henna for Healing

Some henna artists offer henna services with a focus on healing or therapeutic properties. This may involve using special henna blends that are believed to have calming or soothing effects on the skin, or using henna to create designs that are meaningful or symbolic for the individual.
